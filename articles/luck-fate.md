# I Accept Luck Without Surrendering to Fate

## Do luck and fate exist, or are they just stories we tell ourselves to make sense of a chaotic universe?

I have been thinking about the role of luck and fate in our lives for about a week now. Until recently, I did not believe in them. But a conversation with a friend, who is battling a serious illness, forced me to think otherwise.

“I thought hard work would sort out my life”, he told me.

“I studied hard, got into a good college, landed a job that people envied. I worked relentlessly. But look at me now. My health is failing. My life’s saving gone within a year.”

Then he said something that has stayed with me: “How could I not think about luck and fate?”

We plan everything, our education, our careers — forgetting to leave room for luck. But sometimes, our luck catches us off guard. We face a problem that no education or money can solve.

I used to dismiss luck as an excuse and fate as an admission that our choices don’t matter. But since that day, I’ve been questioning my beliefs. His words unsettled me, and I’m still trying to understand why.

### Birth — The First Lottery
The most important event of our life, being born, itself feels like a lottery.

I was born in a middle-class family in Kathmandu. That meant being born to parents who gave equal importance to educating their daughter as to their son. That one simple mindset shaped the entire trajectory of my life. I was given textbooks and pens instead of brooms and mops. The lady who comes to my home to clean is the same age as me, and yet the similarity ends there. For her, instruction was to scrub harder; for me, it was to study harder.

My story did not start with effort. It started with luck.

### Privilege and Efforts — Hand in Hand
I now look back at my own life, and I realise how often luck and effort have worked together in shaping my path.

Years ago, unable to sleep, I searched for a challenging course I could pursue while still spending time with my son. Actuarial Science came up. I had never heard about it. It said you need to be good at mathematics. A few years down the line, I started working as an actuary in a Life Insurance Company, leaving behind the IT education that I once thought was my end goal. This was not something I had planned.

Was it a fate? Or effort? Who was pulling the strings here?

My life started with privilege, opportunities I was born into, but it continued because of my hard work. I turned opportunities into achievements. Without the privilege, there would be no path; without effort, the path would lead nowhere. Today, I believe I am here because of both.

### How Changing Beliefs Have Changed Reality
People who believe in luck accept life’s events more easily than those who don’t. For me, every setback once felt like a personal failure, and every success needed to be justified through merit. I believed nothing came without effort.

But believing in luck has changed my perspective. Now there is no need to map out everything. Outcomes may be beyond my control. So why not take the risks? Knowing that I can still fail despite my best effort, failures seem less upsetting. Also, my success is not merely because of my efforts. There is something that is working in my favour. I understand that there could have been alternate versions of my life where things would be totally different.

I am grateful for all those things that came to me unearned. I have become less judgmental of those who are less fortunate than me, realising that their life is not a reflection of only the choices and efforts they made, but also of the opportunity, timing and the circumstances.

It is humbling to know that many of my achievements were built on the foundation that I did not earn but was blessed with. All the successes were a mixture of privilege, opportunities, chance encounters and effort.

Hard work only pays off if we have a chance first to apply it. And that chance is something we may have no control over entirely.

### Fate vs. Choice: What Really Shapes Us?
But making peace with luck has made me question something else entirely: the idea of fate. I still do not believe in it— I cannot surrender to the thought that everything is scripted. It feels too restricting, too dismissive of the choices that do matter.

To me, events are random, and we make our choices, provided we are lucky to get the chance. One decision leads to another, and our direction for life is shaped. But those events are not predictable, not fated.

Life is a mix of chance and choice, and we navigate it by doing the best we can with what we are given. I accept uncertainty, but I refuse to believe that my life is already written; everything was meant to be.

### Finding Peace in What We Can — and Can’t — Control
The conversation with the friend that first unsettled me is now guiding me.

I still don’t have definitive answers about whether luck and fate truly exist as forces in the universe or merely as useful concepts we’ve invented. But acknowledging their roles in my life has made me more honest, humbler, and hopeful.

Now I am more flexible, ready to adapt when things don’t work out. I celebrate my successes while recognising how much luck shaped them — and how little anyone deserves their misfortunes.

We ought to live knowing our choices matter, but they don’t determine everything. We can prepare for opportunities while accepting we can’t control when — or whether — they arrive. Life is about balancing what we can shape and what we must accept.
