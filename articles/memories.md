# What Are We, If Not Our Memories?

I am ridiculously in love with this song. Every word feels like it is tugging me gently and taking me places. It stirs me, and my past comes alive. At one moment, I am in a bustling street of Sydney, and in a split second, in a chaotic gully of Kathmandu. Just then, I find myself in humid Chennai, immediately coming back to the quiet life in Pune.

I drift from place to place. Each time memories evoke the emotions I had at those moments that are long gone but not forgotten.

I have, since 2000, collected thousands of digital pictures. Whenever I surf through them, I become my memories.

I am the girl with long hair getting ready for my graduation.

I am a seven-year-old trying to dance, oblivious that my skirt is torn.

I am talking to my son in front of the Penna River in Nellore.

And then I come back to my present, folding clothes, still lost in memories.

As the song ends, I realise we not only live in our memories but also live because of them.

We hold on to memories even when life has moved on, relationships ended. We relive them repeatedly, strengthening them each time.

### We Remember, Therefore We Are
Scientifically, memory is a web of neurons that connects our past to our present. Through memory, the brain stores, retains, and recalls information when needed. Memory is what gives us identity. It gives us continuity. We remember being ourselves yesterday, so we are us today. Our experiences, imaginations, and awareness are all stitched together by memories to make us who we are.

Memories give us a path forward. We build skills and knowledge because of them. Our relationships are built on our shared memories. They steer us away from dangers and guide us towards what is safe. They help us make stories of our lives, not a collection of isolated, random moments.

### What Are We Without Memories?
My great-grandmother had dementia, her mind slowly slipping away, taking decades of her life with it. She had forgotten the last forty years of her life. In her mind, she was in a hilly village in remote Nepal. She remembered drying red chillis under the sun. When it rained, she would run outside to save them — only to come back puzzled, not knowing what happened to her chillies. She forgot all of us, her great-grandchildren. She forgot that her husband had died. Took medicines multiple times, not knowing she had already taken them. At times, her memory would come back, breaking her heart. She would cry for a long time, then again, forgetting why she was even crying. Who was she really? Was she the one who forgot or the one who lived fleetingly in the present? Remembering her makes me wonder what memory truly means.

If there were no such thing as memory, what would we be? We would never learn from our experiences. We would live a disconnected life, like my great-grandmother did. We would be strangers to ourselves. We would still perceive the world, feel the emotions, and think, but we would not be able to connect today with yesterday.

### Can We Trust Our Memories?
Memories do not always serve us right. We cling to our past traumas, regrets and guilt, not being able to let them go. Our pains outweigh our joys. We drown in anxiety and depression, not knowing how to channelise those thoughts. And even when the memories are not painful, they still hold us back. Nostalgia makes us remain in the past and resist change. We make our own versions of events as we keep on thinking about them, adding layers of new interpretations over old ones. After a point, the truth blurs, the reality is distorted, and we become biased and prejudiced.

Memory gives us life, but it can also imprison us if we let it.

### Memories- Boon or Bane?
Memories are among the most complex phenomena of the human mind. We cannot always trust or judge reality based on our memories. They are essential to human life. We need them to progress, to carry out daily life. Yet they are also the source of our sorrow. They give rise to both our deepest joys and our most haunting pains. They are the contradictions of a human mind — both a blessing and a burden.

In the movie “A Beautiful Mind”, Nash is brilliant. He builds mathematical theories, but he creates illusions as well. He hallucinates, and those become his memories. This movie shows how fragile the line can be between reality and memory.

Our memories are some real, some distorted. Some beautiful, some broken. Boon and bane. It shapes our reality even when it deceives us.
